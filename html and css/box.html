A tree is a tall, perennial plant with a sturdy trunk, branches, and leaves. It plays a vital role in maintaining oxygen, absorbing carbon dioxide, and providing shelter to various species. Trees help prevent soil erosion, purify the air, and offer shade and beauty to our surroundings. From fruit-bearing trees to towering evergreens, each type contributes uniquely to the ecosystem. Beyond their environmental benefits, trees also hold cultural, spiritual, and economic importance in human life. Protecting and planting trees is essential for a healthier and more sustainable planet.

A cloud is a visible mass of tiny water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the atmosphere. Clouds form when moist air rises, cools, and condenses around dust or other particles in the air. They play a crucial role in Earth's weather and climate, influencing temperature, precipitation, and even the amount of sunlight that reaches the ground. There are many types of clouds, such as cumulus, stratus, and cirrus, each with unique shapes and characteristics. Beyond their scientific role, clouds often inspire art, imagination, and beauty in the natural world.
